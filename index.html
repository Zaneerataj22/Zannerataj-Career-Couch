Mistakes I Made as a Fresher

Real lessons — so you don't repeat them

01

Memorizing answers instead of understanding

I used to memorize interview answers word-by-word. In the actual interview, I forgot mid-sentence and panicked. The fix? Understand the concept, then speak in your own words naturally.

🎤 Interview
02

Ignoring SQL thinking it's not important

I thought SQL was just basic SELECT queries. But in every support and developer role, SQL is used daily — for debugging, validation, and reporting. Learn it deeply, not just the syntax.

🗄️ SQL
03

Not testing APIs before saying "it works"

Early on I assumed if the code ran, the API was fine. Wrong. I learned to always validate request/response in Postman — check status codes, payload, and edge cases before confirming.

🔌 API
04

Panicking during production issues

First time a production issue hit, I froze. Over time I learned: stay calm, gather info first, check logs, reproduce the issue, then fix. Panic never helps — process does.

🚨 Support
05

Not asking questions when stuck

I used to spend hours stuck on a problem, afraid to ask. I learned that asking the right question at the right time is a skill — not a weakness. It saves time and shows maturity.

💬 Mindset
06

Skipping documentation and comments in code

I thought comments were optional. Then I had to revisit my own code 2 months later and couldn't understand it. Now I always write clear comments and document my work properly.

Salary Negotiation Guide

How to answer CTC questions confidently

💬 "What is your expected CTC?"

"Based on my 1.5+ years of experience in application support, API troubleshooting, SQL, and software development, and considering the industry standard for this role in Bangalore, I'm expecting around [X – X+2 LPA]. However, I'm open to discussion based on the overall package, growth opportunities, and role responsibilities."

💡 Always give a range, not a fixed number. Research Glassdoor/AmbitionBox first.

🔄 Counter Offer Script

"Thank you for the offer. I'm genuinely excited about this opportunity. Based on my research and experience, I was expecting something closer to [higher number]. Is there any flexibility on the base salary or joining bonus?"

💡 Always negotiate — companies expect it. The worst they can say is no.

🎓 Fresher vs Experienced Approach

  • Fresher Focus on learning opportunity, not just salary
  • Fresher Say: "I'm flexible and open to your standard package"
  • Experienced Research market rate on AmbitionBox/Glassdoor
  • Experienced Mention current CTC + 30-40% hike expectation
  • Experienced Highlight specific skills that justify the ask

❌ What NOT to say

  • ✗ "I'll take whatever you offer"
  • ✗ "My previous salary was very low"
  • ✗ "I need at least X or I won't join"
  • ✗ Giving a number before they ask
  • ✓ Always let them make the first offer if possible

Resume ATS Tips

Make your resume pass the ATS filter and reach the recruiter

✅ ATS Do's

  • Use standard section headings (Experience, Skills, Education)
  • Include keywords from the job description
  • Use simple fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
  • Save as PDF or Word (.docx)
  • Use bullet points, not tables or columns
  • Spell out acronyms: REST API, MySQL, React.js
  • Include measurable achievements (improved speed by 30%)

❌ ATS Don'ts

  • Don't use tables, text boxes, or columns
  • Don't use headers/footers for important info
  • Don't use images or graphics
  • Don't use fancy fonts or colors
  • Don't use abbreviations without spelling them out
  • Don't submit as image PDF (scanned)
